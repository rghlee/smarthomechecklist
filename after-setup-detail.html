Why updates matter after setup

A camera may seem fully secure once it is installed, but long-term protection depends on what happens afterward. Updates remain one of the main ways to reduce new security and privacy risks over time.

Update status
Medium concern
This issue matters most after the camera has already been installed, because new weaknesses may be found later and fixed only through app or firmware updates.

What this means

Main concern

If updates are ignored or delayed, the camera may continue running with known weaknesses that already have safer versions available.

What could happen

The device may still appear normal while missing important fixes that reduce avoidable security and privacy exposure.

Why homeowners should care

Connected home devices change over time, and updates are one of the main ways manufacturers respond to newly discovered problems.

Real-life example

A homeowner may keep using the same camera for months without realizing a later firmware release fixed an issue already known to the manufacturer.

What to check

Automatic updates Turn them on if the product supports them so fixes are less likely to be missed.
App and firmware Check both the mobile app and the camera firmware, because each can affect security and privacy settings.
Routine review If automatic updates are not available, check the app or support page on a regular schedule.
Settings after updates Review privacy, alert, and sharing settings after major updates in case something changed.

Warning sign

If the app shows unclear update history, repeated delays, or little visible support information, review the product more carefully and confirm it is still actively maintained.
